Big Bend

  • Big Bend National Park lies in the Central time zone
  • Big Bend was made a national park on June 20, 1935
  • The name Big Bend comes from a large bend in the Rio Grande River along the park boundary.
  • Big Bend National Park lies in the Central time zone

Hot Springs National Park

  • Hot Springs National Park covers a total area of 5,550 acres
  • Hot Springs National Park lies in the Central time zone
  • There is no entrance fee for Hot Springs National Park
  • The area was first protected in 1832 as Hot Springs Reservation.

Redwood National Park

  • Over 1.5 million people visit Redwood National and State Parks annually.
  • The first Redwood trees pre-date humans, spiders, and flowers, first appearing over 240 million years ago during the time of the dinosaurs.
  • Redwood was made a national park on October 2, 1968
  • Redwood National Park lies in the Pacific time zone
